Common question and answer

1. What is the best diet for my pet?

The best diet for your pet depends on its species, age, breed, and health condition. Generally, a balanced diet with high-quality protein, essential vitamins, and minerals is ideal. Consult your veterinarian for a personalized nutrition plan.

2. How often should I take my pet to the vet?

For routine check-ups, pets should visit the vet at least once a year. Puppies, kittens, and senior pets may require more frequent visits for vaccinations, dental care, and health screenings.

3. How can I keep my pet active and healthy??

Regular exercise, interactive play, and mental stimulation are essential for your pet’s well-being. Dogs need daily walks and playtime, while cats enjoy toys and climbing structures. Engaging activities help prevent obesity and behavioral issues.

4. What vaccinations does my pet need?

Core vaccinations for dogs include rabies, distemper, parvovirus, and hepatitis. Cats typically require rabies, feline distemper, and feline leukemia vaccines. Always consult your vet to ensure your pet is up to date on vaccinations.

5. How do I prevent fleas and ticks on my pet?
